# Onboarding Churn Reduction Strategy
## AI Shopify Optimization App

---

# Objective

Reduce early churn (0–3 day uninstall window) by:

1. Delivering immediate visible value
2. Replacing “passive learning” with visible signal collection
3. Anchoring the user to a 30-day goal
4. Creating a 7-day activation loop
5. Making intelligence feel tangible, not abstract

---

# Core Problem

Early users uninstall because:

- No immediate insight
- Value feels delayed
- App appears unfinished
- “Learning in background” feels invisible
- No clear next milestone

The goal is to replace passive perception with visible momentum.

---

# Onboarding Architecture

## Step 1: Goal Anchoring (1 Screen)

**Prompt:**
“What is your primary goal for the next 30 days?”

Options:
- Increase revenue
- Improve ROAS
- Fix conversion rate
- Identify wasted ad spend
- Increase AOV
- Understand funnel drop-offs

Store selection as `primary_goal`.

Purpose:
- Personalizes dashboard
- Frames all insights
- Creates direction

---

## Step 2: Immediate Baseline (Under 30 seconds)

Upon install, show:

### Headline:
“Baseline established for your store.”

### Metrics:
- Current Conversion Rate
- Revenue (Last 30 days)
- Top Traffic Channel
- Largest Funnel Drop-off

Even if derived from simple heuristics.

User must see:
“This app already understands my store.”

---

## Step 3: Visible Signal Collection

Replace language:

❌ “Learning your store in the background”

With:

✅ “Collecting signal from traffic and orders”

Show progress indicators:

- Traffic sources mapped ✓
- Channel efficiency classified ✓
- Funnel baseline calculated ✓
- Drop-off analysis in progress…
- Optimization opportunities detected (0/3)

Progress must update in real-time or staged intervals.

---

# 7-Day Activation Loop

Instead of long roadmap, use staged insight release.

## Day 0 – Baseline
“Your store converts at 1.8%. Industry average: 2.4%.”

## Day 2 – Channel Snapshot
“Meta drives 62% of traffic but only 41% of revenue.”

## Day 4 – Funnel Weak Point
“Biggest drop: Add-to-Cart → Checkout.”

## Day 7 – First Action
“Test promoting [Top Product] above fold. Estimated lift: +X%”

Insights can be progressively unlocked.

This creates anticipation.

---

# Dashboard Structure

## Section 1 – Goal Status
Display:

“Progress toward: Increase Revenue”

With simple metric:
Revenue trend vs baseline.

---

## Section 2 – Intelligence Feed
A running feed:

- Funnel anomaly detected
- Channel imbalance identified
- Revenue concentration risk found
- Product outperforming category average

Make it feel like:
The system is actively thinking.

---

## Section 3 – Action Center

Each recommendation must include:

- Why this matters
- Estimated impact
- “Accept” button
- “Simulate impact” option (optional)
- Undo capability

Execution must feel safe.

---

# Chat Interface (Optional)

Do NOT build generic chatbot.

Instead build:
Insight-driven assistant.

Allowed prompts:
- “Why is TikTok underperforming?”
- “Where am I losing revenue?”
- “What should I fix first?”

Bot responses must:
- Reference real metrics
- Provide clear reasoning
- Suggest one action

Avoid:
“How can I help you?”

---

# Psychological Principles

1. Visible progress > passive intelligence
2. Specific > hype
3. Measurable > abstract
4. Short loop > long roadmap
5. Authority tone > early-stage tone

Never use:
- “Cooking V2”
- “Building crazy features”
- “Compounding later”

Use:
- “Detected”
- “Analyzed”
- “Identified”
- “Estimated impact”

---

# Success Metrics

Track:

- 48-hour uninstall rate
- % of users who reach Day 2 insight
- % who click first recommendation
- Time to first perceived value
- Weekly active installs

---

# Key Design Constraint

If user installs and never speaks to founder,
they must still understand:

- What the app does
- What it has learned
- What it will do next
- Why it matters

Clarity must be self-contained.

---

# Final Principle

Solve Day 1 clarity.

If Day 1 feels intelligent and useful,
Day 30 retention becomes possible.

Retention begins at first impression.
